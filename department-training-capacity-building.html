Training & Capacity Building Department

SALMART Diplomatic Hospitality — “Building competence for diplomatic excellence.”

I. Department Overview

The Training & Capacity Building Department is a specialized arm of SALMART Diplomatic Hospitality, tasked with designing and delivering structured, high-impact training programs focused on diplomacy, protocol, executive leadership, and professional etiquette.

Through tailored workshops, knowledge-sharing forums, and high-level speaker engagements, the department strengthens institutional and individual capacity in line with global standards and African contextual relevance.

II. Core Functions & Services

1. Executive Training Programs

  • Custom training modules for government officials, corporate executives, and protocol officers.
  • Includes simulations, situational role-plays, and case study reviews on diplomatic conduct and official representation.
  • Modules can be delivered in-person or virtually, based on client need.

2. Diplomatic Etiquette & Protocol Workshops

  • Practical sessions covering protocol management, flag etiquette, order of precedence, seating arrangements, dress codes, and diplomatic correspondence.
  • Designed for embassies, ministries, regional bodies, and private sector hosts of dignitaries.

3. Speaker Engagements & Thought Leadership

  • Hosting seasoned diplomats, international relations scholars, and senior officials as guest speakers.
  • Forums, town halls, and fireside chats to bridge theory and practice in diplomacy and governance.

4. Capacity Building for Event & Protocol Teams

  • Skills development for internal and external logistics teams involved in managing summits, VIP visits, and high-level government events.
  • Emphasis on risk management, contingency planning, and multicultural sensitivity.

5. Youth & Emerging Leaders Engagement

  • Workshops targeting youth councils, junior diplomats, student leaders, and interns.
  • Focus on foundational diplomacy, public speaking, global citizenship, and international engagement preparation.

6. Training Material Development

  • Manuals, handbooks, visual guides, and interactive tools for institutional and personal reference.
  • Includes post-training resources, certification documents, and performance assessments.

7. Monitoring & Evaluation of Capacity Outcomes

  • Participant evaluations, skills audits, and progress tracking.
  • Recommendations for follow-up training, refresher courses, and mentorship matching.

III. Objectives of the Department

  • To equip professionals and institutions with the diplomatic, leadership, and protocol skills necessary for global engagement.
  • To standardize the practice of official hosting, international etiquette, and cultural intelligence within the African context.
  • To build a knowledge-sharing platform that connects theory with real-world diplomatic practice.
  • To support capacity strengthening in line with Africa’s growing leadership role in global conversations.

IV. Departmental Structure

  • Director of Training & Capacity Building — Provides leadership, oversees curriculum design, and manages partner engagements.
  • Program Manager – Executive Training — Coordinates custom modules and institutional engagements.
  • Protocol & Etiquette Lead Trainer — Delivers protocol and etiquette-based content and assessments.
  • Youth & Emerging Talent Coordinator — Designs youth-targeted programs and outreach.
  • Content Developer & Evaluator — Prepares training material, monitors progress, and manages reporting.
  • Support Staff / Event Assistants — Handle logistics during training sessions, certification, and coordination.

V. Key Deliverables

  • Training curricula and modules tailored to client needs.
  • Certificate-based workshops and institutional training programs.
  • Post-training assessments and feedback reports.
  • Speaker and facilitator directories.
  • Annual training calendar and evaluation summaries.
  • Protocol handbooks and executive conduct manuals.
